The Best Movies According to Meron Mekuria

My top 5 movies of all time

freedom writers

The movie follows Gruwell, played by Hilary Swank, as she starts her first teaching job at a high school with a diverse student body. The students are divided by racial and cultural differences, and many of them come from troubled backgrounds.

The Pursuit of Happyness

The Pursuit of Happyness" is a 2006 biographical drama starring Will Smith and his real-life son Jaden Smith. It tells the true story of Chris Gardner, a struggling salesman who becomes homeless while trying to provide a better life for himself and his son. Despite facing hardships, Chris lands an unpaid internship as a stockbroker, demonstrating his determination and resilience. The film highlights the bond between father and son and emphasizes the power of never giving up on one's dreams. It is an inspiring and uplifting cinematic experience.

Think like a man act like a lady

The movie follows a group of friends, mostly African-American, who are struggling in their relationships. They turn to Harvey's book for advice and use its tips and strategies to try to improve their love lives.

Obessed

"Obsessed" is a 2009 psychological thriller starring Idris Elba, Beyoncé Knowles, and Ali Larter. The film revolves around Derek Charles (Elba) and his wife Sharon (Knowles), whose lives are upended when a temporary office worker named Lisa (Larter) becomes dangerously infatuated with Derek. As Lisa's obsession escalates, she relentlessly targets Derek, jeopardizing his marriage and safety. Beyoncé Knowles delivers a compelling performance as Sharon, Derek's loyal wife who becomes a central figure in the battle against Lisa's relentless pursuit. "Obsessed" explores themes of obsession, betrayal, and the lengths people go to protect their loved ones, making it a suspenseful and thrilling cinematic experience.

Everybody hates cris

The series is a sitcom centered around the experiences of Chris Rock during his adolescence in the 1980s. It draws inspiration from his memories of growing up in Bedford-Stuyvesant, a neighborhood in Brooklyn, New York, which faced challenges like drug use and gang activity. Despite the tough environment, the show highlights the unity and strong bonds within his African-American family. Additionally, it explores the contrast between his home life and his experiences attending a predominantly white public high school located across town.
